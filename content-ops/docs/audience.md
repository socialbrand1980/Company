# Audience Definition

## Segment 1: Business Owner / Founder

### Profile
Owners of small to growing brands.
They want more awareness, better positioning, and stronger sales.
Many do not yet have a structured marketing system.

### Pain Points

- marketing feels random
- content exists but does not move the business
- hard to know what to prioritize
- unclear positioning
- inconsistent brand message
- low conversion from content efforts

### What they value

- clarity
- frameworks
- practical advice
- strategic direction
- results-oriented guidance

## Segment 2: Marketing Manager / Brand Manager

### Profile
Works inside a company and needs external support for strategy, execution, and optimization.
Often responsible for growth, engagement, leads, and conversion.

### Pain Points

- internal team bandwidth is limited
- campaign execution is fragmented
- content lacks strategic consistency
- reporting exists but insight is weak
- difficult to align creative work with business goals

### What they value

- structured planning
- reliable execution
- strategic thinking
- measurable KPIs
- a capable partner, not just an executor

## Segment 3: Scaling Brand

### Profile
Already has product-market traction and wants stronger brand communication, storytelling, and omnichannel coordination.

### Pain Points

- brand voice is inconsistent
- content volume grows faster than quality control
- campaign ideas are not integrated
- message becomes diluted across channels
- team needs operating rhythm and process

### What they value

- premium positioning
- consistency
- scalable systems
- campaign orchestration
- long-term growth support

## General Audience Rule
Write for decision-makers.
Assume the reader cares about growth, clarity, efficiency, positioning, and business outcomes.
