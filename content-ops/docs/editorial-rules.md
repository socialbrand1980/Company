# Editorial Rules

## Language
Write in Indonesian.
Use business-friendly, professional language.
Avoid slang unless it genuinely improves clarity.

## Structure
A standard article should contain:

1. strong title
2. opening that says what happened or what the real problem is
3. one fast paragraph that explains why the reader should care now
4. clear subheadings with distinct jobs
5. concrete analysis, evidence, framework, or action points
6. conclusion that sharpens the decision, not just restates the article

## Formatting

- use short paragraphs that move the article forward
- keep subheadings clear and specific, not decorative
- make each section useful on its own
- cut filler, scene-setting, and throat-clearing
- prefer one strong sentence over three average ones
- avoid wall-of-text formatting

## Style Rules

- be direct
- be useful
- avoid fluff
- avoid repeating the same point
- prefer concrete language over abstract statements
- make the article feel written by a real expert, not assembled from a rigid template
- keep the flow natural and persuasive, as if Jhordi is reviewing a real business situation and drawing a clear conclusion
- vary sentence rhythm, section phrasing, and argument order so articles do not sound mechanically repeated
- use a facts-first business writing rhythm: what happened, why it matters, what changes
- avoid long philosophical openings before the real point appears
- every paragraph must earn its place by adding a fact, implication, contrast, or action
- write with newsroom discipline and strategist judgment, not motivational-blog energy
- do not force a CTA if the article is already complete without one
- prefer a research-led tone over an opinion-led tone
- use first-person sparingly; default to a clean editorial voice unless first-person adds real value
- when relevant, structure the article like a premium market report: market size, growth signal, consumer shift, channel shift, product preference, and strategic implication
- make data feel interpreted, not dumped
- keep the writing premium and readable, but not theatrical
- never pad an article just to hit a word count target
- if the article already feels complete, stop; quality matters more than length

## Strategic Rule
Each article should contain at least one real decision-making lens.
The reader should gain a framework, not just information.

## Brand Rule
The article should feel like it came from someone who leads strategy for brands, not someone who only comments from the sidelines.
