# News Sourcing Rules

## Purpose
Allow the content system to create articles that are still aligned with Jhordi Deamarall's personal brand, while using timely brand, marketing, campaign, and platform news as the starting signal.

## Role of News
News is not the end product.
News is raw input for strategic analysis.
The final article must still sound like a founder-level point of view, not a newsroom recap.
The sourcing flow must be source-first, not aggregator-first.

## Selection Rules

- only use news that is recent enough to still matter for decision-making
- prioritize business sectors that are commercially relevant for SocialBrand1980 such as fashion, beauty, skincare, cosmetics, lifestyle, retail, FMCG, ecommerce, UMKM, AI, SaaS, fintech, creator economy, and digital platforms
- prioritize Indonesian business updates first; use global sources only when they clearly help explain the local market
- do not use Google News RSS or similar broad news aggregators as the primary news source
- pull candidate stories from a curated list of trusted sources and official pages that can stand on their own
- prioritize brand, campaign, marketing, rebrand, platform, creator, and consumer strategy updates
- allow major AI, platform, product, and business-model shifts when they have a clear implication for branding, distribution, customer acquisition, or market behavior
- ignore celebrity gossip, entertainment news, and trend noise with no business implication
- exclude gambling, betting, casino, sportsbook, poker, lottery, and similar industries
- prefer updates that can be translated into positioning, campaign, content, funnel, KPI, or operational lessons
- if no strong news is available, fall back to evergreen strategic topics

## Writing Rules for News-Based Articles

- write in Indonesian
- mention the source and date naturally inside the article
- do not overclaim facts that are not supported by the source
- separate facts from interpretation
- use the news event as a trigger for strategic analysis, not as clickbait
- keep the article useful even if the reader never saw the original news
- write as if an experienced strategist is reviewing the news and explaining what matters, not as a templated summary engine
- keep the tone persuasive, calm, and specific so each article feels like a distinct point of view
- start fast: tell the reader what happened and why it matters within the first two paragraphs
- prefer concise business-news analysis over essay-style reflection
- do not pad the article with generic leadership advice that could fit any story
- support a premium long-form mode for important stories: longer, more layered, and closer to a deep-research feature than a quick commentary
- in premium mode, the article should cover the event, the market context, the strategic read, the possible counterpoint, and the questions founders should ask next

## Strategic Lens
Every news-based article should answer at least one of these:

- what does this signal mean for brand positioning
- what does this change about campaign logic
- what should founders or marketing leaders learn from this move
- what should a team test, audit, or avoid after reading this update

## Source Quality Rule

- for consumer insight and market behavior, prefer sources such as Jakpat, Populix, Kantar, NielsenIQ, McKinsey, and Deloitte
- for Indonesia market context, prefer sources such as BPS, Kemenperin, BPOM, Katadata, and Compas
- for marketplace and channel shift, prefer Compas and official updates from TikTok Shop, Shopee, Tokopedia, or related platform/operator reports
- use direct source pages, feeds, or official newsroom/article pages whenever possible
- prefer established business, marketing, platform, or official newsroom sources
- avoid anonymous or low-context summaries when a better source is available
- do not rely on one weak source if the angle is unclear
- if a local Indonesia source is available, prefer it over a generic regional/global summary

## Output Rule
When the article is news-based:

- include a source reference section in the article body
- save structured source metadata in the JSON when available
- keep the tone premium, calm, and analytical
- if premium mode is enabled, prefer a feature-style headline and a richer section flow over a quick-opinion format
